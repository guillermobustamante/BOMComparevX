# Mission Control Baseline

## Source of truth

The primary source of truth is the approved Mission Control implementation already visible on:

* `/upload`
* `/results`

If a target component is not visible in those pages, extend the same visual language rather than inventing a new one.

## Aesthetic principles

* Data-first and operational
* Quiet, minimal, and spacious
* Flat surfaces without shadows
* Color used sparingly and meaningfully
* Strong readability through typography and left alignment

## Palette

* App background: `#F4F5F7` to `#F8F9FA`
* Surface background: `#FFFFFF`
* Primary text: `#202124`
* Secondary text: `#5F6368`
* Borders and dividers: `#DADCE0` to `#E0E0E0`

Accent families should stay pale and restrained:

* waiting or pending: pale tan with brown-gold text
* baseline or success: pale green with darker green text
* candidate or action: pale blue with standard blue text
* alert or change: pale purple with darker purple text

## Typography

* Primary UI font: clean sans-serif
* Data font: monospace for filenames, revision codes, part numbers, dense metadata, and compact all-caps labels
* Keep font weights restrained
* Do not use italics

## Layout

* Use wide white cards on the light-gray page background
* Use thin 1px borders and low radii
* Use generous padding and whitespace instead of heavy decoration
* Keep headers and controls aligned to edges
* Keep data left-aligned

## Components

Buttons:

* white background
* thin gray border
* subtle rounding
* dark text

Icon buttons:

* square
* same border and surface treatment as Results

Drop zones:

* 1px dashed border

Inputs and search:

* wide
* flat
* white
* lightly bordered

Badges and tags:

* pill-shaped
* pastel accent background
* 1px border
* matching text color

Tables:

* no vertical lines
* thin horizontal separators
* no filled header background
* subtle header text
* optional pale row emphasis where needed
